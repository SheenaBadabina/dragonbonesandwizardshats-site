# The Thief Who Stole Tomorrow

In Aurathel, where spires kissed clouds and magic sang through stone, there lived a thief named Raven. Not just any thief. The best.

She moved like smoke through moonlight. Like whispers through silence. Like secrets through dreams. Her fingers knew the language of locks, the poetry of tumblers, the songs that bronze and iron sang when properly persuaded. She had stolen starlight from merchant vaults. She had lifted crown jewels from sleeping kings. She had walked through walls of spell-ward and shadow-trap and emerged untouched, unscathed, unknown.
